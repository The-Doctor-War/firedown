Read: B buys the title a second line (≈22→≈40 chars) without losing any tiles-per-screen, by parking mime+fact in a corner pill that has its own contrast. Its one wrinkle is the bottom-right corner: the title's 2nd line has to reserve that column (a right-pad) so text never slides under the badge — manageable, but it means the 2nd line is a bit narrower than the first. C is strictly more legible (title on solid ground, no scrim) but it's a different grid — taller tiles, fewer per screen, less "wall of images." If the grid's job is browsing media at a glance, B keeps the density and still doubles the title; if reading titles is the priority over seeing images, C wins. A stays the safe, shipped baseline. My lean: B — unless you find the corner pill fighting the art on busy thumbnails, in which case C.
