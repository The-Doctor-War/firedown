My pick: A. It fixes the actual problem — the toggle and its count are the same concept, so they belong in the same row. Halves the row count, removes the two duplicated icons, and keeps one clear drill-in affordance (the count pill) distinct from the toggle. Connection + Clear data stay as a separate "manage" pair (genuinely different actions).

B if you want the sheet to lead with a confident trust number (nice for a privacy-first browser), at the cost of a taller sheet. C if you want maximum density but it muddies tap targets (whole row drills, but it also holds a toggle).

Note on the toggle↔count interaction in A: the row has two targets — the count pill (drill into detail) and the switch (toggle protection). That's a deliberate split; tapping the label area does nothing or could mirror the pill. Worth deciding.
